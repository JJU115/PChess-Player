Overview

This program is a game of Chess coded in the Java language. It features a fully interactable GUI to allow play
between two people. Improvements/additions are being made on a regular basis to enhance the user experience.
This page offers a rudimentary background and explains the rules of the game. Click the 'Back' button if you
wish to return to the Welcome screen or the 'Play' button if you wish to begin a new game, both are located
at the bottom of this screen.


How to Play


Interacting with the GUI

Clicking the 'Play' button on this page or the Welcome page will take you to the game screen
which displays the chess board and pieces. The label in the top right of the screen will display the
current player's turn (White or Black) and the label directly below will display the current player's
piece selection, if the label is blank then no piece has been selected. The current player can select
the piece they wish to move by clicking on it, if it appears in the piece selection label then the
selection has been registered.

With a selected piece, the current player may move it by clicking on the square of the board they
wish to move it to. If the move is valid then the piece will be relocated to that square and
the player's turn ends. If, before making a valid move, a player wishes to cancel their
choice of piece to move then they may click the currently selected piece and the selection icon
will go blank allowing the player to choose another piece.
Play will continue until one of three conditions is met discussed in the next section 'Ending the Game'.

The player controlling white pieces always has the first move and each player must on their turn make a
valid move until play ends.
Any move which puts a player's King in danger is deemed illegal.



Ending the Game

The game can end in three different ways:

  • Win/Lose
  • Stalemate
  • Quit/Resignation


Win/Lose

Either player may win the game by placing the other player's King in Checkmate.
This is when a player is in Check and no move on that player's turn allows them to escape Check.


Stalemate

Stalemate occurs when the player whose turn it is to move is not in Check but every possible move
places them in Check.


Quit/Resignation

Either player may end the game by clicking on the 'Quit' button located on the bottom right of the
game screen. They may also end the program by exiting the graphical window containing the game screen.



Pieces and Moves

Pieces

Pawn

The Pawn may move 1 square directly in front of it towards the opposing player's side provided it is
unoccupied, a Pawn may not move backwards. On its first move only the Pawn may move 2 squares forward
provided both squares are unoccupied. Pawns may capture enemy pieces that are 1 square diagonally in front
of them. Pawns can perform 1 of 2 special moves: En-Passant and Promotion.


Rook

The Rook may move any number of squares along the same row or column but cannot jump over other pieces.
The Rook can perform the Castling move along with the King.


Knight

The Knight moves in an 'L' shape: 2 squares vertically and 1 square horizontally or 2 squares horizontally
and 1 square vertically. The Knight is allowed to jump over other pieces.


Bishop

The Bishop may move any number of squares diagonally but cannot jump over other pieces.


Queen

The Queen may move any number of squares along the same row, column, or diagonal.
Thus the Queen combines the movement of the Rook and Bishop.


King

The King can move 1 square in any direction. The King may perform the Castling move with the Rook.



Moves

En-Passant

If player A's Pawn moves 2 squares on its first move and player B's Pawn is directly adjacent on
the horizontal then B's Pawn may capture A's Pawn En-Passant (In Passing) where B moves to the
very last square A passed over. B must do this on the turn directly after A and A must have double jumped
otherwise it is deemed illegal. Player B would select their Pawn and click the last square that A's Pawn
passed over.


Promotion

If a Pawn moves onto 1 of the squares on the last row on the opposite end of the board from where it
started then that Pawn as part of its move may be promoted into a Queen, Rook, Knight, or Bishop of
the same color. There is no restriction on which piece the Pawn can be promoted to so it is possible
to have 2 Queens, 3 Rooks, etc...
Once a Pawn reaches the end of the board the moving player will be presented with a menu prompting
them to select their promotion piece.


Castling

Once per game the King is allowed to Castle, moving 2 squares towards a Rook on the same row
and moving that Rook to the last square the King passed over. Castling is permissible under the
following conditions:

  • Neither the Rook or King have moved previously during the game.
  • There are no pieces between the Castling King and Rook.
  • The King is not in Check, does not pass through any squares under attack by enemy pieces,
    and doesn't land in Check.

Castling is permissible however if the Rook is under attack or passes through a square under attack.
To Castle, select the King and click the square 2 spaces away on the same row in either direction.
